What is Autism?

a condition related to brain development that impacts how a person perceives and socializes with others, causing problems in social interaction and communication.

How does Music Therapy help Autistic people?

The literature reports that most individuals with ASD respond positively to music. People with ASD often show a heightened interest and response to music, making it an excellent therapeutic tool for working with them

Music therapy helps individuals with ASD identify and appropriately express their emotions. Because music is processed in both hemispheres of the brain, it can stimulate cognitive functioning and may be used for remediation of some speech/language skills.

Research has shown that music therapy can help children develop or improve skills like shared attention, communication and play. It might help autistic children and children with intellectual disabilities more than typically developing children.

What kind of music is best for autism?

Music therapy for autistic people is often improvisational. This means the music is spontaneous and matches the temperament of the person playing.

Music therapists say music with a strong beat, simple structure, and easy lyrics do best.

According to Autism Connect, songs that may help with autism include:

  • "Here Comes the sun" by the beatles
  • "Shake It Off" by Taylor Swift
  • ("Sittin' On the Dock of the Bay") by Otis Redding

The Limitations of Music Therapy for Autism

Finding the Right Tune: Considerations for Choosing Music Therapy for Autism

When considering music therapy for individuals with autism, it is important to find a therapist who has experience working with this population. Additionally, it is important to consider the individual’s preferences and needs when selecting music activities. Some individuals may respond better to certain types of music or instruments, and it is important to tailor the therapy to their individual needs. Finally, it is important to be patient and persistent with music therapy, as progress may not be immediate and may require ongoing support.
